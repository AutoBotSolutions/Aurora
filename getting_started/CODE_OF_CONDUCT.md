# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in the **G.O.D. (Generalized Omni-dimensional Development)** community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- Age
- Body size
- Visible or invisible disabilities
- Ethnicity
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Education
- Socioeconomic status
- Nationality
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Religion (or lack thereof)
- Sexual identity and orientation

We are committed to fostering an environment where everyone feels welcomed, included, safe, and respected.

---

## Our Standards

To create a positive and inclusive community, the following behaviors are expected from all members:

### Examples of Behavior That Contributes to a Positive Environment:
- **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
    - Recognize and respect cultural, linguistic, and personal differences.
- **Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences**
    - Embrace diversity of thought without undermining others' contributions.
- **Providing constructive feedback kindly and respectfully**
    - Frame critiques in a way that is actionable and considerate of the individual's perspective.
- **Gracefully accepting feedback**
    - Feedback should be seen as an opportunity for learning and growth—not as an attack.
- **Focusing on what’s best for the community and the project**
    - Communicate intentions clearly when suggesting changes or addressing different priorities.
- **Showing empathy toward others in the community**
    - Understand that everyone comes from unique backgrounds.

### Examples of Behavior That Is Unacceptable:
- **The use of sexualized language or imagery**
    - This includes jokes or comments that create an uncomfortable or intimidating environment.
- **Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments**
    - Targeting gender, sexual orientation, race, religion, disability, or other protected attributes.
- **Trolling, personal attacks, or inflammatory comments**
    - Deliberately inciting conflict or provoking others.
- **Unwelcome physical or verbal attention**
    - Behavior that makes others feel unsafe or objectified is unacceptable.
- **Publishing others' private information**
    - This includes sharing personal contact details without explicit permission.
- **Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting**

---

## Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing these standards, both in the codebase and within any public forums associated with the project, such as:
- GitHub Issues
- Pull Requests
- Project Chat/Discussion Rooms (if available)

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported directly to the project team at:  
**`youremail@example.com`**

### Steps We Will Take:
1. **Acknowledge Receipt**
    - We will acknowledge reports within **24-48 hours**.
2. **Investigate**
    - All complaints will be reviewed fairly and privately, and actions will be determined based on the context of the reported behavior.
3. **Confidentiality**
    - We will respect the confidentiality of the reporter and any individuals involved.
4. **Action**
    - If a violation of the Code of Conduct is determined, we may take any of the following actions, depending on severity:
        - Issue a verbal or written warning.
        - Require the offender to attend conflict resolution training.
        - Temporarily or permanently ban the individual from participation in the project and its community spaces.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to **all project spaces**. These include:
- GitHub repositories
- Issue tracking systems
- Pull request discussions
- Social media related to the project
- Any public or private online or offline spaces where the project is discussed by its community

In addition, this Code of Conduct applies **in public spaces** when an individual is representing the project or its community.  
Examples include:
- Attending conferences or meetups in connection with G.O.D.
- Public communications mentioning the project

Behaviors in these situations that violate this Code of Conduct may also negatively affect the community environment and may result in enforcement actions.

---

## Reporting Violations

If you observe or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to:
**`support@autobotsolutions.com`**

To assist us in addressing your report effectively:
- Detail the behavior that occurred.
- Specify where it occurred (e.g., issue comment, private message).
- Include any supplementary material (links, screenshots, etc.) to illustrate the behavior.

Your privacy and anonymity will be respected to the fullest extent. If needed, we will follow up with you to gather further details.
